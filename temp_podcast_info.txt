Reflection on Existing AI in Education Podcasts
After reviewing a wide range of educational podcasts on AI, including those focused on classroom use, pedagogy, ethics, policy, daily news, district strategy, and teacher roundtable, it is clear that we have a different POV that is missing from the conersation. Of course, most are "Global minority dominant" without any type of POV from minority voices. Given that around 70% of the students we teach are of color within the public school system, we need to have a Podcast that comes from where we are now and where we should be going into the future with technology.

Yes AI (because it is the buzz word), but we need to cover all disruptive technology now and in the future.  While informative, these tend to offer only a single point of view and often lose energy or depth after the initial exchange. Simple interviewing and droning on as in the first podcast from New Zealand, was super boring after about 10-15 minutes. The second podcast on the list was backed and funded well, and had a flow, but I was tired of the two hosts after about 30 minutes. I listened to each all the way through. I feel tht we need to have a blend of us going through current events, etc and then hering from experts who are making change. This will bring tangible steps to where we are, whats the current discourse, explain and even grapple with the information, and then move into having actionable plans for leaders to move forward on the topic.

Our podcast must be distinct: more dynamic, more representative, and deeply aligned with who we are and the communities we serve.
